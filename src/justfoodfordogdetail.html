My First Interview Experience & What I Learned

My first interview didn't go so well. I went to a pet store called JustFoodForDogs. I chose this store because I found that there were a lot of pet owners in this community, and there were also many pet-related shops around, so I thought I might find something from this perspective.

In retrospect, the interview was not well prepared. First, my approach is abrupt and unnatural. I'm too nervous myself. I should be more relaxed. Then, my questions were too limited to the topic of pets, and I didn't do a good job of bringing the conversation to the community level. And I didn't ask if I could record it.

Through this experience, I found that doing interviews is really skillful. The questioner needs to be able to ask the right questions to open the conversation, to change the subject when the other person is unable to continue, and to have coherence and depth between questions.

The choice of interviewees is also important. Maybe some people are less talkative themselves, or they really don't have anything to say about my questions. These are all factors I need to consider before my next interview.

key takeaways

Common Customer Questions

Many customers inquire about ingredients, especially grain content in pet food. They also seek recommendations for specific pet needs such as puppies or older dogs with sensitive stomachs.

Interview Approach Learnings

Future interviews should include:

  • Better preparation and natural conversation starters
  • Questions that expand beyond specific topics to community-level insights
  • Permission for recording the conversation
  • Flexibility to adapt when interviewees are less responsive
